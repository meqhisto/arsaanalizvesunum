## 2025-04-24 - [Avoid Cartesian Products in SQLAlchemy Analytics Queries]
**Learning:** In `utils/query_optimizer.py`, performing multiple `outerjoin`s on one-to-many relationships (e.g., joining User to both ArsaAnaliz and Contact tables simultaneously) and using `func.count()` produces a Cartesian product. This multiplies the counts (e.g. 5 tasks and 4 contacts results in counts of 20 for both) and slows down the database severely ($O(n^k)$ where $k$ is the number of joined tables).
**Action:** Always use separate `.count()` queries (when filtering by a single entity like `user_id`) or correlated scalar subqueries (`select(func.count(Table.id)).where(...).correlate(BaseTable).scalar_subquery()`) when needing to aggregate over multiple independent one-to-many relationships for a single root entity.
